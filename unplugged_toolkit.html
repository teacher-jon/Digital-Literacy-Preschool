Unplugged Prop Toolkit

Teach Digital Citizenship Offline Using Analog Materials

The Goal: You do not need real devices to teach 3-to-4-year-olds digital citizenship. Use this list of physical classroom props to embed digital concepts gracefully into dramatic play, sorting activities, and circle time.
📱

Cardboard "Devices"

Used For: Transitions, Gentle Hands & Taking Turns

Create completely inert tablets or smartphones using cardboard, or use old phones with the batteries removed. Place these in the dramatic play center.

  • Practice physically handing a device gently to a friend.
  • Practice putting the device in a designated "sleep box" when a transition timer rings.
  • Learn the vocabulary of asking for a turn.
🖼️

Empty Picture Frames

Used For: Faces First (Relationships)

Use wooden or plastic picture frames with the glass removed.

  • Have children look at each other face-to-face through the empty frame.
  • Practice making eye contact and reading facial expressions.
  • Reinforce that the people in the room are more important than what’s on a screen.
🃏

Sorting Picture Cards

Used For: Real vs. Pretend (Media Literacy)

Laminating cards showing two versions of an object: a high-quality photograph of the real object and a cartoon/illustrated version of it.

  • Sort real animals versus cartoon animals from popular shows.
  • Ask questions: "Can this dog talk to us like the one on TV?"
  • Helps children begin distinguishing the physical world from fictional media logic.
🛑

"Stop" & "Ask" Badges

Used For: Ask A Grown-Up (Privacy & Safety)

Wearable badges or physical stop signs on a popsicle stick that are placed near any classroom screen (like the teacher’s computer or a smartboard).

  • Children practice the physical action of holding up the stop sign and looking for a teacher.
  • Reinforces the absolute boundary condition that children never touch digital interfaces without an adult present.
