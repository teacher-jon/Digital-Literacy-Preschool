"What Would You Do?" Scenario Cards

Cut these cards out and use them during the workshop to spark discussion. How can we use Total Physical Response (TPR) and simple routines to handle these moments?

Transitions & Time

"During center time, a student refuses to put the listening station iPad away when the visual timer goes off, and starts crying."

Discussion Prompts

  • What physical action (TPR) might help them let go?
  • Is there a transition song we can introduce?
  • How could this connect to a "How We Organize Ourselves" unit?
Real vs. Pretend

"A child sees a cartoon dog on a tablet and insists it's a real pet that can come live in the classroom with them."

Discussion Prompts

  • How can you distinguish 'media world' from 'real world' using dramatic play?
  • Can we build a sorting activity out of this?
  • How does this connect to our 'Where We Are in Place & Time' unit?
Ask a Grown-Up

"A child wants to play with the interactive whiteboard and keeps randomly tapping the screen before you are ready for the lesson."

Discussion Prompts

  • What is the simple, repetitive vocabulary to correct this? ("Hands in lap", "Ask first")
  • What offline game could practice this boundary?
  • Which Learner Profile attribute fits best here? (e.g. Principled)
Faces First

"You are trying to speak to a student, but they are completely transfixed staring over your shoulder at a peer watching a video."

Discussion Prompts

  • What is a physical cue/action we can practice to redirect attention to faces?
  • Why is teaching "faces first" critical in the digital age?
  • How can we model this during Morning Meeting?
Kind Words & Turns

"Two students are fighting over an unplugged toy cell phone in the dramatic play center, acting out a 'gaming' scenario."

Discussion Prompts

  • How can we use offline toys to practice online sharing?
  • What vocabulary can we give them to "ask for a turn"?
  • How does this shape their understanding of cyberbullying later on?
Identity / My Trusted Adults

"An ad pops up on an educational game, and a student gets frustrated and starts hitting the tablet to make it go away."

Discussion Prompts

  • What physical action can we teach instead of 'hitting' or trying to click it themselves? (e.g., Raise hand, say 'Oops!')
  • How do we normalize digital "accidents"?
  • Who are the 'trusted adults' in this classroom they can ask?
