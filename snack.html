S.N.A.C.K.

A Preschool-Specific Framework for Digital Literacy Mapping

S

Simplify the Concept

Take the abstract, older-grade concept (e.g., Cyberbullying, Digital Footprints, Screen Time Management) and strip it down to its most foundational, offline social-emotional core.

N

Name the Habit

Give the specific behavior a simple, repetitive 2-3 word phrase that a 3-year-old can easily memorize (e.g., "Faces First", "Ask a Grown-up", "Gentle Hands").

A

Anchor to the PYP

Explicitly map the simplified habit to your current Transdisciplinary Theme (e.g., Who We Are) or a specific Learner Profile attribute (e.g., Principled, Caring).

C

Center Play

How can this habit be practiced safely using unplugged toys in the dramatic play center? (e.g., using toy phones to practice turn-taking, or sorting cards for 'real vs. pretend').

K

Kinesthetic Response

What is the Total Physical Response (TPR)? Define the exact muscle memory or physical body action the child will do to reinforce the habit (e.g., Hands in lap, covering ears, handing a transition token to a teacher).

Example: Mapping "Media Literacy & Fact vs. Fiction"

  • S (Simplify): Telling the difference between the screen world and the real world.
  • N (Name): "Real vs. Pretend"
  • A (Anchor): Where We Are in Place & Time (Understanding our physical world).
  • C (Center): A sensory bin sorting activity with plastic real animals vs. printed cartoon animals.
  • K (Kinesthetics): Having children physically point to their own eyes and then point to the object when deciphering "real" vs "cartoon".
